Business Problem

The insurance company cannot easily analyze its policyholder base and claim patterns because policy and claims data are scattered across multiple sources. This makes it difficult for stakeholders to track key metrics, identify risk trends, and understand customer segments. As a result, the company lacks clear insights needed to make data-driven decisions on risk management, claims, and policy strategy.

Data & Modeling Approach

  • Implemented a star schema with insurance_data_car as the central fact table
  • Created multiple dimension tables including Age, Age Group, Car Color, Car Make, and Car Model for detailed analysis
  • Established one-to-many relationships between the fact table and dimension tables using relevant keys
  • Developed DAX measures for Total Claim Amount, Average Claim Amount, and demographics like Kids Driving and Gender Counts

Key Insights

  • The portfolio consists of 37,541 policies with a total claim amount of approximately $187.8M, resulting in an average claim amount of about $5,003 per policy and an average claim frequency of 0.51 claims per policy. This indicates a relatively stable portfolio with moderate loss exposure but opportunities to improve profitability through targeted risk segmentation.
  • Exposure concentration shows that Private car use accounts for about 80% of policies and nearly 80% of total claim value. This suggests that most improvements in portfolio performance should focus on optimizing pricing and underwriting strategies within the Private segment.
  • Customer distribution by education indicates that policyholders with a Bachelor’s degree represent roughly 50% of policies and claim value. While this may not directly influence risk, it highlights where the largest customer base lies, which can guide marketing and retention strategies.
  • Risk segmentation analysis shows variations across groups. Urban coverage zones show slightly higher claim amounts per policy (around $5,038), households with three kids driving have higher claim frequency (about 0.54 claims per policy), and drivers under 25 demonstrate higher claim frequency compared to the portfolio average.

Recommendations

1. Pricing and Underwriting Improvements

The company should prioritize risk-adjusted pricing strategies within the largest exposure segments, particularly Private car use policies.

Rather than increasing premiums broadly, pricing adjustments should focus on risk characteristics associated with higher losses, including:

  • Number of Kids Driving in a household
  • Age Group, particularly drivers under 25
  • Coverage Zone, with special attention to Urban areas

2. Product and Risk Management Strategies

For higher-risk customer segments (for example, Urban areas combined with young drivers and multiple kids driving), the company can introduce risk-reduction measures beyond pricing.

Potential actions include:

  • Offering higher deductible options
  • Implementing telematics or usage-based insurance programs for youthful drivers
  • Providing driver safety programs or safe-driving discounts tied to driving behavior

3. Portfolio Growth and Customer Retention

Since the portfolio is heavily concentrated in the Private segment, most growth and retention efforts should focus there, but with data-driven targeting.

The company should:

  • Focus customer acquisition on segments with lower claims per policy
  • Retain profitable customer groups through targeted incentives rather than broad discounts
