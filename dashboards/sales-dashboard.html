Business Problem

The car dealership lacks a centralized system to monitor and analyze sales performance across different car models, regions, colors, and body styles. Without clear visibility into key metrics such as total sales, average price, and cars sold over time, management struggles to track trends, evaluate performance, and make data-driven decisions to improve sales growth. A dynamic Power BI dashboard is needed to provide real-time insights and support strategic decision-making.

Data & Modeling Approach

  • Implemented a star schema with car_data as the central fact table
  • Created a Calendar dimension table containing Date, Week, Month, and Year for time-based analysis
  • Established a one-to-many relationship between the Calendar table and car_data using the Date field
  • Developed DAX measures for Total Sales, Cars Sold, Average Price, and Year-over-Year (YOY) growth

Key Insights

  • The dataset contains 23,906 car sales generating approximately $671.5M in total revenue, with an average sale price of about $28.1K. This highlights the large operational scale of the dealership and a strong overall sales base.
  • Revenue distribution across dealer regions is relatively balanced, but Austin leads with $117.2M from 4,135 units at an average price of about $28.3K. Other strong regions include Janesville ($106.4M) and Scottsdale ($96.0M), while the remaining regions generate between $87M–$89M. Austin’s leadership appears to be driven primarily by higher sales volume rather than pricing.
  • Brand performance analysis shows that Chevrolet leads revenue with $47.7M and an average price of about $26.2K, indicating strong unit sales. Ford follows with $47.2M but at a higher average price of about $29.3K, suggesting stronger pricing power. Dodge ranks third with $44.1M, highlighting different competitive strategies across brands.
  • Product mix analysis highlights key revenue-generating models including Lexus LS400 ($14.26M revenue, ~$40.3K average price), VW Jetta ($12.29M), Oldsmobile Silhouette ($12.27M), and Mitsubishi Montero Sport ($11.47M). Premium vehicles such as Cadillac Eldorado (~$41.9K average price) also demonstrate strong performance in the luxury segment.
  • The sales data reveals two successful product patterns: premium vehicles with high average prices and moderate volume (e.g., Lexus LS400, Cadillac Eldorado) and mainstream vehicles with strong sales volume and mid-range pricing (e.g., VW Jetta, Oldsmobile Silhouette). These patterns suggest different marketing and sales approaches may be required for each category.
  • Customer income analysis shows that vehicle purchase prices remain relatively consistent across income groups (approximately $27.8K–$28.5K) despite significant differences in income levels. This suggests that factors such as financing options, inventory availability, or brand preference may influence purchasing decisions more than income.
  • Time trend analysis shows strong revenue growth in early 2022, increasing from approximately $8.9M in January to about $22.7M by April. This may indicate seasonal demand patterns or increasing market activity as the year progresses.

Recommendations

1. Regional Sales Strategy

The dealership should analyze the drivers behind Austin’s strong sales performance and replicate similar strategies in other regions where possible.

  • Identify operational or marketing practices contributing to higher sales volume in Austin
  • Apply successful sales tactics and promotions across other underperforming regions
  • Improve inventory allocation to match demand patterns across regions

2. Product Segmentation Strategy

Since the data reveals two strong product segments—premium vehicles and mainstream high-volume vehicles— sales strategies should be tailored accordingly.

  • Promote premium vehicles through targeted marketing focused on luxury features and brand prestige
  • Drive volume for mainstream models using promotions, financing options, and broader advertising campaigns
  • Maintain a balanced inventory between premium and high-volume vehicles

3. Revenue Forecasting and Planning

Given the observed upward revenue trend in early 2022, the company should adopt time-based forecasting methods to better predict sales performance.

  • Use rolling monthly or quarterly forecasts instead of relying on early-month sales results
  • Monitor seasonal demand patterns to optimize marketing campaigns and inventory planning
  • Adjust sales targets based on historical growth trends and demand cycles
